How can I support my child's development?


Steps, once your child has a diagnosis


  1. Thanks to education and awareness to this growing condition, now children with special needs are identified as early as 18 months.
  2. Consult a good Developmental Specialist-your child may have been diagnosed with expressive, receptive speech delay , PDDNOS, Autism, ADHD, Cerebral palsy, Mental Retardation or a Learning Disability. Your doctor will guide you in regard to the therapy, medication and parenting approach.
  3. Have a vision for your child-Chalk out a plan, include your doctor, speech therapist, Occupational therapist and Special Educator.
  4. Have simple achievable goals towards Developmentment, starting with what the child is able to do already. This will motivate the child and prevent frustration, for the parent/therapist.
  5. Depending on the diagnosis your child may need a number of therapies, speech, O.T, special education, Sensory Integration. Having a schedule will make things easier; children thrive on routine, so try and keep, meal-times, nap time as predictable as possible.

What can I do to help?

In my experience, empowering parents to work with child is as, if not more fruitful than adjunct therapies. Parents if trained and positive make the best therapists. They are with the child, 24/7, every moment becomes a teaching moment. While training parents, I highly recommend an eclectic approach-a combination of Behavioral therapy and Floor time.
